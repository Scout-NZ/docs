Tō mātou kaupapa · Our kaupapa

The way we walk alongside

Who we are, what anchors us, how we practise, and how we are governed. Te Awhi is anchored by Te Taupunga, guided by our Moemoeā, Kaupapa, and Uara, and delivered through the four Ora priorities of our 2025-2030 Strategic Plan.

Ko wai mātou · Who we are

Two rohe, one kaupapa

Te Awhi Whānau Charitable Trust is a kaupapa Māori mental health and addictions provider in Aotearoa. We were founded in Kirikiriroa in 1994, commenced in Whangārei in 1996, and have grown into a steady presence across Waikato and Te Taitokerau.

Today we have 89 kaimahi, two offices, and seven service pathways that walk alongside whānau wherever they are on their journey, supported by a base of owned whare in both rohe.

Te Taupunga · The anchor that holds us

Whakatō te kākano, ko wai au, mirimiri ki te aroha, tipua te ngākau Māori, i roto i te Wairua Tapu.

Plant the seed, who am I, nourish it with love, nurtured with our ancestral gifts, encompassed in the Holy Spirit.

Te Taupunga sits at the centre of everything we do. It is the anchor stone our kaupapa is built on, and the place every plan, decision, and conversation comes back to.

Direction

Moemoeā and Kaupapa

Moemoeā

Standing proud in our own skin

Ka tū pakari au me tōku kiri, ki te taha o tōku iwi.
Our Moemoeā is for every whānau member we walk alongside to stand grounded in who they are, with their people around them. We hold tika, pono, and aroha at the heart of this work.

Kaupapa

From the anchor stone, we build the road

Mai i te Taupunga, ka hanga huarahi, hei whai mā tātou.
Our Kaupapa is to build pathways from Te Taupunga so whānau can flourish in their own way, at their own pace.

2025-2030 Strategic Plan

Four Ora, one kaupapa

Our Strategic Plan, set by our Board and brought to life through our operational plans, organises the mahi around four Ora priorities. Each Ora carries outcomes for whānau and for Te Awhi.

Whānau Ora

Strong, healthy whānau across generations. From trauma to healing, with equity of access, quality information, and the resources to thrive.

Mauri Ora

A flourishing life force. Self-management, agency, dignity, spiritual wellbeing, and strength in te ao Māori, free from racism and discrimination.

Wai Ora

Connection to whenua, whānau, hapū, iwi, and the resources every whānau needs. Good housing, safe environments, and clear pathways to support.

Te Awhi Ora

Organisational wellbeing. Strong teams, sound systems, kaupapa-focused partnerships, financial security, and cultural safety for kaimahi.

Awhi Taea · Our practice model

Awhi means to embrace. Taea means it can be done.

Awhi Taea is how our kaupapa shows up in practice. It is the model we have built over three decades of walking alongside whānau, drawn from kaupapa Māori principles, Enabling Good Lives, and peer leadership.

Tino rangatiratanga

Tū Whānau lead their own journey. We walk alongside.

Mana motuhake

Self-determination is the goal of every plan, not a side benefit.

Manaakitanga

Care, respect, and hospitality are non-negotiable, every day.

Te reo me ōna tikanga

Te reo Māori and tikanga are woven through the mahi, not added on top.

Kaitiakitanga

We hold responsibility for the people, the whenua, and the resources entrusted to us.

In practice

Awhi Taea is not theory. It is the good life plan written in plain English with the person, not about them. It is going at the pace of the person, not the pace of the contract.

Wellbeing framework

Wellbeing as a whole house

Te Awhi works within Te Whare Tapa Whā, the model developed by Tā Mason Durie in the 1980s. Wellbeing rests on four walls: taha tinana, taha hinengaro, taha wairua, and taha whānau. When one wall is weak, the others carry more weight. Our mahi tends to all four.

How we measure

Mana, voice, and tino rangatiratanga

Lived experience sits inside Te Awhi's leadership, not on the side of it. We listen to Tū Whānau through the Tū Whānau Council. We measure our mahi against three things whānau told us to measure: Mana (what they feel), Voice (what they say), and Tino rangatiratanga (the lead they take in their own life).

Tō mātou hītori · Our story

Founded in 1994. Still walking alongside.

  1. 1994 · KirikiriroaTe Awhi Whānau Charitable Trust was set up because whānau in Kirikiriroa needed a kaupapa Māori option for mental health support. The founding intention was simple: walk alongside people with mana, in their own language and tikanga, for as long as they needed.
  2. 1996 · WhangāreiThe mahi grew north because the need was there. Today our Whangārei team carries the same kaupapa, in the same way, alongside whānau in Te Taitokerau.
  3. Three decades of growthWe added residential support so whānau had a safe place to live. We added respite so people had somewhere to land. We added community and outreach so the mahi could meet people in their own homes. We added Raranga Tahi alongside Te Whatu Ora, and Tipu Ora and Kāinga Rua to support the steady step back into everyday life.
  4. The next chapterOur current direction focuses on strengthening practice, supporting Kāinga Rua to keep doing what it does best, deepening whānau voice through the Tū Whānau Council, and growing kaimahi capability. The next decade will look different from the last. The kaupapa will not.

Governance and leadership

Held by the people who hold the kaupapa

Te Awhi Whānau Charitable Trust is governed by a board, supported by a senior leadership team led by our Tumuaki, and held in relationship with our Tū Whānau Council.

Our board

Our governance board holds the strategic direction and accountability of the Trust.

Tumuaki and senior leadership

Te Awhi is led by Tumuaki Laurie Hakiwai and an experienced senior leadership team. Lived experience sits inside our leadership, not on the side of it.

Tū Whānau Council

The Tū Whānau Council is the formal way that whānau voice shapes Te Awhi. Council members are elected by Tū Whānau who use our services and bring lived experience to governance and practice decisions.

Charitable status and reporting

Te Awhi Whānau Charitable Trust is a registered charity. Annual reports and financial statements are available on request.

Partnership and governance enquiries

We work in partnership with Te Whatu Ora and other government agencies, commissioners, philanthropic funders, iwi, hapū, and kaupapa Māori organisations. For partnership or governance enquiries, contact the Tumuaki's office.
