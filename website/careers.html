Why mahi with Te Awhi

The kaupapa is grounded, the mahi is real

Te Awhi has been walking alongside whānau in mental health and addictions for over thirty years. You will be part of a team that knows what whānau need, because they have walked alongside thousands.

We are a kaimahi whānau. Mahi with us is real, sometimes hard, and always kaupapa-driven. We invest in our people because the people hold the kaupapa. If you are values-aligned, we want to hear from you, even if there is no current role advertised that suits.

How we look after our people

Real support for kaimahi

Pay that respects the mahi

Starting rate $28.95 an hour for kaiawhina, well above the sector average, with an enhanced KiwiSaver employer contribution.

Health and wellbeing

$500 HealthNow card topped up monthly, Employee Assistance Programme, and access to mirimiri and chiropractic care.

Growth and supervision

Cultural and professional supervision shaped with each Tū Mahi, and training pathways: kaupapa Māori, clinical, and leadership routes into senior roles.

The culture

Our kaupapa, lived daily

Awhi Taea, embracing possibilities, is not just a model. It is how we work, every day. Tino rangatiratanga, mana motuhake, manaakitanga, te reo me ōna tikanga, and kaitiakitanga show up in supervision, in team meetings, and in the moment a Tū Mahi turns up to a whānau home.

Lived experience is welcome here

Many of our kaimahi bring their own lived experience of mental distress, addiction, or whānau journeys alongside someone they love. That experience is a strength. We will work with you on safe practice, supervision, and the supports that suit you.

What we look for

Tū Mahi who bring respect for whānau, openness to te ao Māori, and an honest commitment to do good mahi. Formal qualifications are useful and not always required.

Where the mahi happens

Kirikiriroa (Hamilton, head office) and Whangārei (Te Taitokerau). Residential, respite, community outreach, acute community alternatives, services for tamariki and rangatahi, and the front door of Awhi Mai.

Honest about the sector

Mental health and addictions mahi is hard, and the sector is under pressure. We are honest about that. We also know that strong kaupapa, strong supervision, and strong supports change what is possible.

Te Awhi

Te Awhi Whānau Charitable Trust is a kaupapa Māori mental health and addictions provider working alongside whānau in Kirikiriroa and Te Taitokerau. For more than thirty years, we have walked with whānau and the people who love them.
