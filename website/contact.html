For clinicians and referrers

What helps a referral start well

Most Te Awhi services are accessed through clinical referral pathways; Te Whānau Pātaka welcomes direct contact from whānau. We do not need everything to be perfect, and we can talk through what is missing.

  1. The person's details, with consent

    Name and contact details for the person being referred, with their consent where possible.

  2. What is happening for them

    A brief description, any existing supports (clinical or community), and any safety considerations we should know about.

  3. Who to come back to

    Who is making the referral, and the best way to contact you. We respond within a working day and keep referrers updated where consent allows.

Feedback and complaints

We want to hear it, good or hard

If something has not gone well, or something has gone especially well, please tell us. Use the form above and choose “Feedback or complaint”, phone either office, or write to us. Complaints are handled with care, without judgement, and without any effect on the support you receive.
