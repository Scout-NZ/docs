Outcomes and evidence

Outcomes that whānau themselves can see

Our outcomes framework was built with whānau, for whānau. It measures three things tū whānau told us to measure: Mana (what they feel), Voice (what they say), and Tino rangatiratanga (the lead they take in their own life).

Hua Oranga and He Puawai

Hua Oranga is a Māori-developed outcome measure that asks the person, their whānau, and the clinician how things have shifted across Te Whare Tapa Whā. He Puawai is a complementary tool for tracking progress over time. We are exploring both, alongside Te Whatu Ora-aligned reporting.

Independent evaluation

A 2024 evaluation of Raranga Tahi (Waikato) was completed by Whakatātūtika (Tautika). A developmental evaluation is underway, jointly sponsored by Te Awhi and Te Whatu Ora Te Tai Tokerau. Public summaries will be published here once approved for release.

What we will not measure away

Some of the most important shifts cannot be reduced to a number. A whānau member returning to the marae after years away. A rangatahi back at kura. A tū whānau saving for the first time. We hold the qualitative alongside the quantitative.

How we use the data

Outcomes data sits inside our governance cycle. It informs practice change, kaimahi development, and annual planning. We report against shared sector measures with Te Whatu Ora and other funders.

Te Awhi

Te Awhi Whānau Charitable Trust is a kaupapa Māori mental health and addictions provider working alongside whānau in Kirikiriroa and Te Taitokerau. For more than thirty years, we have walked with whānau and the people who love them.
