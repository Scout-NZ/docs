Awhi Whānau

A home, with people who know. Residential support, around the clock, in a setting that is built to feel like home.

KirikiriroaResidential · 24/7Clinical referral
How to get support Talk to us

Live-in support, kaupapa Māori, 24/7

Awhi Whānau is for people who need a safe home with consistent support as they navigate mental distress or addiction. The houses are staffed around the clock, the kaupapa is steady, and the days are built around what each whānau member needs.

Who lives here

People who need more support than community services can give right now, and who would do best in a home setting rather than a clinical one. Some come in straight from acute care. Some come in after respite. Some come in from a whānau home that needs a break.

Days that look like life

Meals together. Good life plans worked through with the person, not about them. Time to rest and time to move. Connection to community, work, learning, and culture wherever it fits. Rongoā is available. Time at the marae is encouraged where appropriate.

As long as it takes

There is no set length of stay. We work alongside the person and their whānau to know when they are ready to step into something different.

A home that lives the kaupapa

Awhi Whānau is where Te Whare Tapa Whā shows up at home. The four walls are tended together. Tinana, hinengaro, wairua, and whānau, every day.

Me pēhea te tono · How to get support

Getting started with Awhi Whānau

  1. Kōrero with your clinical team or GP

    For this service, your clinical team, GP, or current provider starts the referral. If you do not have one, talk to us first and we will help you find the right door.

  2. The referral comes to us

    We do not need everything to be perfect. The information that helps most: the person's name and contact details (with their consent where possible), a brief description of what is happening, any existing supports, and any safety considerations we should know about.

  3. We respond within a working day

    A kaimahi will pick up the kōrero, listen, and work out together what next step makes sense. We will be honest if Te Awhi is not the right fit, and we will help you find one that is.

Referrals come through Awhi Mai, a clinician, or another provider.

Te Awhi

Te Awhi Whānau Charitable Trust is a kaupapa Māori mental health and addictions provider working alongside whānau in Kirikiriroa and Te Taitokerau. For more than thirty years, we have walked with whānau and the people who love them.
