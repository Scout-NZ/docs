Our services

Raranga Tahi

Weaving together: peer-led transition support in Kirikiriroa, and an acute community alternative in Whangārei, in partnership with Te Whatu Ora.

Two kaupapa, one name

Raranga Tahi is named for the act of weaving together: people, places, kaupapa Māori practice, and the whānau around the person. In Kirikiriroa it is a peer-led service walking alongside whānau moving from Awhi Mai back into the community of their choice. In Whangārei it is an acute community alternative for whānau in serious distress who would otherwise be admitted, delivered with Te Whatu Ora.

Kirikiriroa: your good life, your way

The Tū Tautoko Aropā (peer leaders) walk alongside you with their own lived experience, supporting you to set your kaupapa using Te Whare Tapa Whā and track how you are going with Hua Oranga. Enabling Good Lives principles sit at the heart: self-determination, person-centred, mana enhancing.

Kirikiriroa: who it is for

Whānau who are residents of Awhi Mai and preparing to transition back into the community of their choice. You can self-refer, or your key worker can refer with your agreement.

Whangārei: a safer way through

A high level of presence, in the place the person feels safest, for people experiencing acute mental distress who can be safely supported in community rather than admitted. Tikanga and rongoā are part of the mahi, not an add-on.

What we have learned

A 2024 evaluation of Raranga Tahi (Waikato) was completed by Whakatātūtika (Tautika). A developmental evaluation is underway, jointly sponsored by Te Awhi and Te Whatu Ora Te Tai Tokerau. Findings will be shared once approved for public release.

Me pēhea te tono · How to get support

Getting started with Raranga Tahi

  1. Kirikiriroa: self-referral from Awhi Mai

    As a resident of Awhi Mai you can make a self-referral, or your key worker can refer with your agreement. If a referral is deferred, the reasons are discussed openly and worked through together.

  2. Whangārei: referral through Te Whatu Ora

    Referrals come from acute and community mental health services, supported by a shared assessment. Contact us directly to discuss whether Raranga Tahi may be appropriate.

  3. A clear plan for what comes next

    Raranga Tahi in Whangārei is a time-limited acute intervention. Length is determined by clinical and whānau review, always with a clear plan for the next step.

Not sure which Raranga Tahi applies? Talk to us and we will point you right.
