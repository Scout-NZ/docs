Our community front door

Awhi Mai is where most people start. Some whānau come to us directly. Some come through a clinician, a kaupapa Māori provider, or another service. Some come because someone they love asked us for help. Wherever you are coming from, Awhi Mai is the team that picks up the phone and starts the kōrero.

Who can use this service

Awhi Mai is for whānau living with mental distress or addiction, their whānau, and the people supporting them. We do not need a formal diagnosis to talk. We can also help you understand whether another service of ours, or another organisation, would be a better fit.

What happens when you reach out

A kaimahi will sit with you, listen, and ask the questions we need to ask. From there, we work out together what next step makes sense: short-term community support, respite, residential, peer support, or a connection to a service outside Te Awhi.

What you can expect from us

We will return your contact within a working day. We will treat your information with care. We will be honest if Te Awhi is not the right fit, and we will help you find one that is. We will not put you through a long process before you meet a person.

Awhi Taea in practice

The way Awhi Mai works reflects our kaupapa. Tino rangatiratanga means you lead. Manaakitanga means you are welcomed. Mana motuhake means the plan is yours.

Me pēhea te tono · How to get support

Getting started with Awhi Mai

  1. Reach out however suits you

    Call us, email us, or send the contact form. You can get in touch for yourself, for someone you love, or as a referrer.

  2. We start the kōrero

    A kaimahi will listen first and ask the questions we need to ask. No pressure, no rush, no queue without a person.

  3. We find the right fit together

    That might be a Te Awhi service, or a better fit elsewhere. Either way, you will leave with a next step.

Awhi Mai responds within one working day.
