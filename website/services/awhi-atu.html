Our services

Awhi Atu

Support where you are. Community and outreach mental health and addictions support in Kirikiriroa.

KirikiriroaCommunity · outreachClinical referral
How to get support Talk to us

Walking alongside, in everyday life

Awhi Atu provides community and outreach support for whānau living in their own homes or whatever they call home right now. We meet people where they are, on the days and times that work, and we focus on the practical mahi of building a steady life.

Who this service supports

Whānau living with mental distress or addiction who want support without leaving their everyday life. People stepping down from residential or respite. People who do not want to come to an office, or for whom an office is not the right setting.

What support looks like, week to week

A consistent kaimahi who knows you. Good life plans built with the person. Time spent on the things that matter: rent, kai, work, learning, hapori connections, mokopuna, marae. Connection to clinical care, peer support, and rongoā as needed.

Awhi Atu and Awhi Taea

Awhi Atu is Awhi Taea in motion. Tino rangatiratanga and mana motuhake show up in the everyday: the kaimahi works to your plan, not theirs.

Part of a bigger continuum

Awhi Atu often follows residential or respite support, holding steady contact as independence grows. It connects with every other Te Awhi service.

Me pēhea te tono · How to get support

Getting started with Awhi Atu

  1. Kōrero with your clinical team or GP

    For this service, your clinical team, GP, or current provider starts the referral. If you do not have one, talk to us first and we will help you find the right door.

  2. The referral comes to us

    We do not need everything to be perfect. The information that helps most: the person's name and contact details (with their consent where possible), a brief description of what is happening, any existing supports, and any safety considerations we should know about.

  3. We respond within a working day

    A kaimahi will pick up the kōrero, listen, and work out together what next step makes sense. We will be honest if Te Awhi is not the right fit, and we will help you find one that is.

Most whānau come in through Awhi Mai or a clinician.
