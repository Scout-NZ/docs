Tiaki Mai

A place to land. Short-term respite for whānau and the people around them, in a home setting with steady support.

Respite, the kaupapa Māori way

Tiaki Mai is a short-term stay for whānau who need a break, a circuit-breaker, or some time away from a hard situation. The setting is a home, not a hospital. The support is steady, and the kaupapa is the same as the rest of Te Awhi.

Who comes for respite

People living with mental distress or addiction who would benefit from a few days or weeks of supported time in a calm setting. People stepping down from acute support. Whānau supporters who need to rest while their person is in good hands.

What to expect during a stay

A welcome on arrival. A kōrero about what you want from the stay. Meals, quiet, the rhythm of a home. Kaimahi who are present without being intrusive. Connection to other services where it helps.

How long people stay

Stays are short. The exact length depends on the person and is agreed up front so it is clear for everyone.

Why respite is part of Awhi Taea

Sometimes the most useful thing is a pause. Tiaki Mai reflects manaakitanga and Te Whare Tapa Whā: a place where the four walls can be looked after gently, for as long as is helpful.

Me pēhea te tono · How to get support

Getting started with Tiaki Mai

  1. Most stays are arranged by a clinician

    Most stays are arranged through a clinician, a needs assessment service, or another provider working alongside the person.

  2. Whānau can also reach out directly

    If you think respite might help you or your person, talk to us. We will help you work out whether Tiaki Mai fits and what the pathway looks like.

  3. We agree the stay together

    Length and purpose are agreed up front with the person and their whānau, so it is clear for everyone.

Tiaki Mai supported 138 people through respite in the last year.

Te Awhi

Te Awhi Whānau Charitable Trust is a kaupapa Māori mental health and addictions provider working alongside whānau in Kirikiriroa and Te Taitokerau. For more than thirty years, we have walked with whānau and the people who love them.
