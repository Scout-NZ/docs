Growing back into the everyday. Community participation support across Kirikiriroa (Tipu Ora) and Whangārei (Kāinga Rua).

Two services, one kaupapa

Tipu Ora in Kirikiriroa and Kāinga Rua in Whangārei walk alongside whānau who are stepping back into the everyday. Both services are grounded in the same kaupapa, with practice tailored to each rohe.

Who these services support

People who are ready to take steady steps into community participation: into mahi, learning, hapori, marae, financial confidence, or home life. People who want kaupapa Māori support to do that.

What support looks like

A consistent kaimahi who knows you. Group and one-to-one mahi. Connection to community: kapa haka, hip-hop, māra kai, learning bus routes, getting back to driving, financial mentoring, work and study pathways. Steady company through the parts that feel hardest.

Kāinga Rua, two homes

Kāinga Rua means two homes, recognising that for many of us home sits in more than one place. The kaupapa is about whakapapa, hapori, and the steady mahi of building a life across the places that matter.

Real shifts

Tipu Ora has grown from supporting 28 whānau to supporting 55. Many of the whānau on the Kāinga Rua kaupapa are saving for the first time. Tū whānau have moved from acute care into steady community life.

Me pēhea te tono · How to get support

Getting started with Tipu Ora and Kāinga Rua

  1. Kōrero with your clinical team or GP

    For this service, your clinical team, GP, or current provider starts the referral. If you do not have one, talk to us first and we will help you find the right door.

  2. The referral comes to us

    We do not need everything to be perfect. The information that helps most: the person's name and contact details (with their consent where possible), a brief description of what is happening, any existing supports, and any safety considerations we should know about.

  3. We respond within a working day

    A kaimahi will pick up the kōrero, listen, and work out together what next step makes sense. We will be honest if Te Awhi is not the right fit, and we will help you find one that is.

Referrals come in through Awhi Mai, your clinician, or another provider.
