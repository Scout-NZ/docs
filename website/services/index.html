How access works

Most services come through a clinical referral

Te Awhi services such as Awhi Whānau, Tiaki Mai, Awhi Atu, Tipu Ora, Kāinga Rua, and Raranga Tahi sit alongside clinical mental health services. Access happens through clinical pathways so the right kaupapa-grounded support meets the right person at the right time, in partnership with the clinical team already alongside them. Te Whānau Pātaka is the exception: whānau can contact us directly.

For whānau

For most services, your clinical team or GP starts the referral. For Te Whānau Pātaka, you can contact us directly. If you are not sure where to start, talk to us and we will help you find the right door.

Talk to us

For clinicians and referrers

We are clear about what we offer. We respond within a working day. We are honest if Te Awhi is not the right fit, and we will help find someone who is. We keep referrers updated where consent allows.

What helps a referral start well

Ready to take the first step?

Awhi Mai will respond within one working day, talk through what is going on, and help find the right next step, whether that is one of our services or a better fit elsewhere.
