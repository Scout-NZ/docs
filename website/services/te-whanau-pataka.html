Walking alongside tamariki, rangatahi, and their whānau

Te Whānau Pātaka is a kaupapa Māori service that supports tamariki and rangatahi, and the whānau around them. We meet young people on their terms, we work with the whole whānau, and we keep tikanga at the centre.

Who this service supports

Whānau with tamariki and rangatahi living with ADHD or related neurodevelopmental challenges, and whānau who would benefit from kaupapa Māori support to walk alongside young people and the people who love them.

What support looks like

Group programmes designed for whānau and rangatahi. Whānau by whānau work, alongside the family unit in the everyday. Whānau hui where it helps. Time with a kaimahi who is the right fit. Time on the whenua and in the activities that grow rangatahi.

Privacy and safety

We take the privacy and safety of tamariki and rangatahi seriously. We work within New Zealand privacy law and our own kaupapa around consent and whānau participation.

Tikanga, whānau, and the next generation

Te Whānau Pātaka holds the kaupapa for the next generation. Tikanga, te reo, hapori, marae, and the whānau around the rangatahi all shape the mahi.

Me pēhea te tono · How to get support

Getting started with Te Whānau Pātaka

  1. Contact us directly, or through a referrer

    Te Whānau Pātaka supports self-referral: whānau can reach out directly. Referrals also come through Awhi Mai, schools and kura, clinicians, and other providers.

  2. We meet, we listen

    A kaimahi who is the right fit will sit with you and your whānau, listen, and work out together what support looks like.

  3. The plan belongs to the whānau

    Group programmes, one-to-one time, whānau hui, or a mix. The pace and shape are set with the whānau, not for them.

This is the one Te Awhi service where whānau can contact us directly, no clinical referral needed.

Te Awhi

Te Awhi Whānau Charitable Trust is a kaupapa Māori mental health and addictions provider working alongside whānau in Kirikiriroa and Te Taitokerau. For more than thirty years, we have walked with whānau and the people who love them.
