He āwhina ināianei | Urgent help

  • Call 111 if you or someone else is in immediate danger.
  • Call or text 1737 to talk with a trained counsellor, free, any time.
  • For specialist mental health crisis support, contact your local crisis team.

These services are available 24 hours a day, every day. You can also talk to us during office hours.

Accessibility

This website is for everyone

Here is how we try to make that real, and what to do if something is not working for you.

We want every whānau member to be able to use this site, whatever device, connection, or assistive technology they use. This site is built to WCAG 2.1 AA: semantic landmarks and headings, a skip link, visible focus states, colour contrast checked on every pairing, text that scales, and full keyboard access. The site works without JavaScript, and animation is switched off for people who prefer reduced motion.

It is also built for real-world phones and connections: pages are small, and nothing stands between you and the contact and crisis pathways.

If anything on this site is hard to use, or does not work with your assistive technology, please tell us. We will fix what we can, and find another way to get you the information in the meantime.

Te Awhi

Te Awhi Whānau Charitable Trust is a kaupapa Māori mental health and addictions provider working alongside whānau in Kirikiriroa and Te Taitokerau. For more than thirty years, we have walked with whānau and the people who love them.
