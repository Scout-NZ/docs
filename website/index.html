Why we exist

Mental health and addictions support, done the kaupapa Māori way

When people face mental distress or addictions, mainstream systems do not always meet them with mana. Te Awhi was set up in 1994 so whānau in our rohe would have a kaupapa Māori option. Three decades on, that kaupapa is still the centre of everything we do.

Read about our kaupapa

Awhi Taea · Embracing possibilities

Awhi means to embrace. Taea means it can be done.

Awhi Taea is how we walk alongside. It is grounded in Te Whare Tapa Whā, in tikanga, in lived experience, and in the Awhi Taea principles that hold our mahi steady: tino rangatiratanga, mana motuhake, manaakitanga, te reo me ōna tikanga, and kaitiakitanga.

Real outcomes

What changes when we walk alongside

When whānau are walked alongside with mana, real change follows. Tū whānau have come off insulin, moved from acute crisis to steady community life, learned to use buses independently, joined hip-hop groups, and started saving for the first time.
